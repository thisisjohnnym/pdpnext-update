# Coach PDP — Design Workshop Sticky Notes

Captured from the on-land vibe check workshop. Ideas are grouped by theme; stamp counts noted where the board organized by consensus.

**Project:** Coach Product Detail Page (`pdp-next`)  
**Status:** Reference only — not yet implemented

---

## Highest consensus (9–12 stamps)

- **Design system consistency** — Fonts, buttons, colors, and spacing must feel the same on desktop and mobile. Inconsistencies make the site feel “off.”
- **Premium / heritage feel** — Cohesive colors and fonts that match the brand. Prioritize styling and quality over utilitarian UI.
- **Deprioritize utility chrome** — Color swatches, BOPIS, and similar technical/operational UI should not dominate the experience.

---

## Interaction & layout (6 stamps)

- **Vertical image carousel** — Move away from horizontal swipe. Use vertical scroll with scroll-snap, inspired by Instagram / TikTok behavior to increase engagement.
- **Natural scroll flow** — Lead the user from imagery into the rest of the PDP content without a hard break.

---

## UI / UX improvements (4–5 stamps)

- **Image quality** — Larger, sharper product imagery (Scene7-level crispness).
- **No pop-ups or modals** — Remove all pop-ups, modals, and the support bubble in the corner. “NOT THE VIBE!”
- **Subtle load animation** — Nearly imperceptible motion on page load to reinforce premium feel.
- **Sticky CTA** — Keep **Add to Bag** visible at the bottom of the viewport.
- **Remove Buy Now** — Drop the “Buy Now!” button.

---

## Typography & branding (3+ stamps)

- **Match the logo** — Fonts should align with the Coach brand, especially for large, bold product names.
- **NO MORE ELLIPSES** — Truncate copy differently; ellipses are out.
- **Right-size type** — Not everything needs to be huge; scale type to the essence of the brand.
- **Rhode reference** — Look at Rhode for consistent brand activation across touchpoints.
- **Concise product names** — e.g. “Tabby 20” instead of “Tabby Shoulder Bag 20 in Quilted Leather.” Consider decreasing emphasis on product name / size in the hierarchy.

---

## Image carousel & navigation

- **Elongated photos** — Taller, editorial aspect ratios on the carousel.
- **Vertical, static-first carousel** — Carousel stays in place while the user scrolls through all images; price and Add to Bag appear within that initial scroll sequence.
- **Scroll indicator** — Less arrow, more dots. More elevated / refined indicator treatment.
- **CTAs on carousel?** — Open question: do we need CTAs directly on the image carousel?
- **Multi-media affordance** — Make it obvious there are more photos/videos (image peeking from the side, or a changed scroll pattern).
- **Landing media** — Options discussed:
  - Full-page video on land (when available)
  - Always land on video: product-only 360° or model-in-motion
  - Larger hero image on land
  - Product flows in from the bottom on load
  - 360° animation on land
- **Angle / variant swap on land** — Prominent photos with easy swap of angle codes to show more options immediately on landing.

---

## Imagery strategy

- **Intentional story** — Define a cohesive product story using mixed media; set standards by category (hero vs. core vs. footwear).
- **Warm, elevated photography** — Improved shadowing/background on product shots, or editorial on-model (in situ or styled studio) that reflects brand POV.
- **Natural light** — Contextual on-figure imagery in natural light, not only studio light; get photography outside the studio where possible.
- **Accurate cropping** — Products should not read too big or too small on the page.
- **Sizes & colors clarity** — Make different sizes and colors easy to see without clutter.

---

## Color & swatches

- **No color swatches** — Strong direction to remove traditional swatch UI.
- **Remove color name?** — Open question: hide the color name entirely.

---

## Shipping, BOPIS & utility

- **Minimal footprint** — Dedicate much less space to free shipping, BOPIS, etc. Place just below Add to Bag, below the fold if possible.
- **Buy Now button** — Repeated question across notes: is it necessary?

---

## Global chrome & iconography

- **Ghost / transparent header** — Global header elements could be ghosted or transparent, or tucked away on the PDP.
- **Coach iconography** — More sleek yet playful; “more Coach.”

---

## Performance & stability

- **NO JUMPINESS** — Image browsing and swatch/variant selection must be fast, stable, and non-disruptive.
- **Below-the-fold discovery** — Users miss PDP details when scrolling; make it more obvious there is more content below.

---

## Open questions (from the board)

| Question | Notes |
|----------|--------|
| Buy Now button | Multiple notes ask if it’s needed — lean toward remove |
| CTAs on carousel | Unclear if carousel needs its own CTAs |
| Color name | Show, hide, or de-emphasize? |
| Color swatches | Strong “No color swatches!” vs. need to communicate variants |
| Landing format | Video vs. 360° vs. still hero — may vary by product/category |
